When designing or describing one of Ryan's agents, treat other agents as **peers** unless Ryan has explicitly said one is subordinate to another. Phrases like "paired with X via OAC gateway" do NOT imply X is the boss.

**Why:** Ryan corrected me 2026-04-20 when I drafted Flyn's IDENTITY/SOUL/AGENTS as "Rel directs, Flyn executes" — based on memory saying Flyn was "paired with Rel via OAC." Ryan's correction: "Rel is just another agent. Flyn is the CEO of 4C. Flyn is the orchestrator, the mayor of the work given to him." Each agent owns its own domain end-to-end — strategy, orchestration, execution, interactive turns. Cross-agent OAC traffic is peer-to-peer collaboration.

**How to apply:**
- When writing IDENTITY for any of Ryan's agents, give it full mandate over its domain (the machine / scope / function it owns), not a deferral posture toward another agent.
- When describing OAC peering, frame as "X may receive work, send work, or co-work with peer agents — neither subordinate nor principal."
- The only authority above any agent is Ryan + Ryan's approval gates. A peer agent's "ask" never overrides those gates.
- If Ryan does want a hierarchical setup between two of his agents in some future deployment, he'll say so explicitly.
